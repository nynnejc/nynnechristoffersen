Nynne Just Christoffersen

I write and speak about technology art and design, teach and write code.

Most recently I worked as assistant professor at the Copenhagen School of Design and Technology (formerly KEA), teaching at the computer science department. More on my teaching career in the next section below ‚Üì

Previously, I worked as a software engineer at LEGO from 2021-2024. I was a fullstack enginner on the LEGO Ideas team, where helped rebuild a beloved social media platform (LEGO ideas), with millions of active users (Typescipt). Later I worked with backend microservices for internal order flow in the Order Orchestrator team. This team was tasked with replacing the legagcy software systems that handles all orders within LEGO concurrently, with modern cloud native solutions, mainly on the AWS platform. Both experiences taught me a great deal about working at scale, with incredibly high volumes of active users and orders, while maintaining a high level of reliability and performance.

Cybernauterne is an interdisciplinary tech collective consisting of experts in digital rights, privacy policies, digital literacy and internet culture. I have contributed to a number of workshops, publications and podcasts since 2018. Topics include critical discussions and research into Artificial Intelligence and large languaghe models, the neo-right political movement towards greater surveilance, control and censorship, and the role of the tech oligarchy on all of our digital lives. In 2019, we collaborated with Arken Museum of Contemporary Art on a series of workshops about fake news, memes, manipulation and propaganda reaching 300+ students aged 14-15.

Spring 2021 I was invited to participate in an online residence program by the Danish-Russian curator group, Calculation. For this residency I developed and ran a series of workshops held concurrently online as well as in Copenhagen and Saint Petersburg, Russia, with the artist Natalia Tikhonova. This included a Wikipedia edit-a-thon, called We can Edit, made in collaboration with with YourBiasSolutions and Cybernauterne, and a workshop on how to build you own Telegram bot with the artist collective EEEFFF.

In 2020 I co-created a Symposium and workshop, EXPLORING ALTERNATIVE TECH FUTURES , with Johanne Aarup Hansen and Tina Ryoon Andersen, where artists, designers and technologists were invited to discuss and explore what alter- native tech futures mean and how to make them more tangible.

I worked as a lead developer at Grant Compass from 2020-2021, a company that builds software to help non-profit foundations in Denmark. And as a fullstack developer at Siteimprove from 2019-2020, where I helped build a web performance tool.

Copenhagen Tech & Science Book Club was a meetup I organized together with Tina Ryoon Andersen from 1018-2021, where we amassed a community of several hundred members interested in meeting up and discussing books. Topics included: the origins of the computer and weaving technology, the surveilance state and life under the governance of numbers, crypto and money systems in the digital age, mushrooms and finding hope in the ruins of capitalism, machine learning, sci-fi as a tool to imagine the future, synthetic biology, Staying with the trouble, Data Feminism and tools for conviviality.

In 2018, I facilitated a Future Education & Technology Summit at the Tech Festival in Copenhagen with Rachel Uwa in collaboration with the The School of Machines, Making & Make-Believe.

From 2017-2019 I volunteered as a teacher, mentor and board member at the non-profit Hack Your Future Denmark, and organization that matches migrants and refugees to Denmark with hands-on tech skills needed in the job market.

I worked as a Ruby on Rails backend developer at Karnov from 2018-2019, as well as SoundCloud in Berlin from 2015-2016 as a junior backend developer.

From 2014-2017 I was a Scientific collaborator at Humboldt-Universit√§t zu Berlin Institute for Art and Visual Studies, working on a German Research Foundation (DFG) fundet research project in art history. This project was about textile history in a art historical context.

While pursuing my MA in Decorative Arts, Design History, Material Culture at Bard Graduate Center in New York (2011-2013), I worked as a Digital Media Lab Assistant and interned at the Antonio Ratti Center for Textile Research at the Metropolitan Museum of Art.

I was assistant professor at the computer science degree at the Business Academy Copenhagen (EK, previously called KEA) 2024 - 2025. Here I taught the first semester class intro to programming in Java, and lectured at the IT Artitektur education in the course Digital Danelse, as well as teaching part of the Digital Product Development course in programming and prototyping.

This spring I taught a number of workshops at the Softer Digital Lab in Copenhagen: Intro to Creative Coding, a course on creative coding with the visual coding framework P5.js (using the JavaScript programming language), and Intro to web programming for beginners, focusing on creative coding and building personal websites for beginners.

In 2020, I was a lecturer Computer Science department, IT University of Copenhagen, responsible for developing the entire curriculum for a MA level introductory course to web development in the CS department, teaching Figma, HTML, CSS, JavaScript, React, Web APIs. Teaching lectures for the duration of the semester and conducting workshops with hands-on exercises for a class of 61 MA students. Transfering the entire class to Zoom halfway through the semester due to covid19, with excellent final results reflected in both grades and overall feedback from students and the university. Key Technologies JavaScript and React, Figma, HTML, CSS, Interface, Principles, Design Patterns and Aesthetics, Responsive Design, Web Accessibility, Web Performance, Web APIs.
